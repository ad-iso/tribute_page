Alan Mathison Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Here's a timeline of his life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, United Kingdom
  • 1922 - 1926 - Attended Hazelhurst Preparatory School
    • 1926 - Attended Sherborne School
  • 1927 - Turing continued to show remarkable ability in the studies he loved, solving advanced problems in 1927 without having studied even elementary calculus.
  • 1928 - Aged 16, Turing encountered Albert Einstein's work; not only did he grasp it, but it is possible that he managed to deduce Einstein's questioning of Newton's laws of motion from a text in which this was never made explicit.
  • 1931 - 1934 - After Sherborne, Turing studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge, where he was awarded first-class honours in mathematics.
  • 1936 - Turing published his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, United Kingdom
  • 1936 - Awarded Smith's prize
